Percy Jackson is a fictional character created by American author Rick Riordan. He is the protagonist of the Percy Jackson and the Olympians series, as well as a major character in The Heroes of Olympus and The Trials of Apollo series. Percy is one of the most beloved characters in modern young adult fantasy literature.

== Origins and Creation ==
Percy Jackson was created by Rick Riordan, who invented the character to tell bedtime stories to his son Haley, who had been diagnosed with dyslexia and ADHD. Riordan used Greek mythology as the backdrop, explaining that Percy's learning differences were actually signs of a demigod heritage. The character's full name is Perseus Jackson, named after the Greek hero Perseus, one of the few heroes in Greek mythology who had a happy ending. Riordan chose this name deliberately as a good omen for the character.

The first book, The Lightning Thief, was published in 2005. It became an instant bestseller and launched one of the most successful young adult fantasy franchises in history.

== Percy's Identity and Background ==
Percy Jackson is the son of Poseidon, the Greek god of the sea, and Sally Jackson, a mortal woman. This makes Percy a demigod — half human, half god. He was born on August 18, and grows from age 12 in the first book to age 17 by the end of The Last Olympian.

Percy grew up in New York City, raised by his mother Sally and his abusive stepfather Gabe Ugliano (whom he eventually turns to stone using Medusa's head). Sally Jackson is Percy's most important human relationship — he describes her as the best person he knows. She later marries Paul Blofis, a kind English teacher Percy grows to respect.

Percy attended Yancy Academy in upstate New York before his life changed forever, and later attended Goode High School in Manhattan during his teenage years.

== Percy's Dyslexia and ADHD ==
Percy has dyslexia and ADHD, which are explained in the books as natural traits of demigods. His dyslexia exists because his brain is hardwired for Ancient Greek, not English. His ADHD is actually his battle reflexes, keeping him constantly aware of his surroundings during combat. These traits, which made Percy feel like an outcast in the mortal world, are reframed as strengths in the world of Greek mythology.

== Personality ==
Percy Jackson is known for his sarcastic, witty sense of humor. He narrates his own story in first person with a casual, self-deprecating voice that makes even terrifying situations feel approachable. Despite his reputation as a powerful hero, Percy consistently underestimates himself and is genuinely humble about his abilities.

Percy is fiercely loyal — loyalty is arguably his defining trait. He would sacrifice himself for his friends and family without hesitation. This loyalty is so extreme that the goddess Athena considers it his fatal flaw: Percy would risk the entire world to save the people he loves.

Percy is also stubborn and rebellious toward authority, particularly toward gods who treat mortals or demigods as pawns. He has talked back to Zeus, argued with Ares, and defied the Olympian Council on multiple occasions. Despite this, he has a strong moral compass and always tries to do what is right.

== Abilities and Powers ==
As a son of Poseidon, Percy has extensive water-based powers:

- Hydrokinesis: Percy can control water in all its forms — directing ocean waves, creating water shields, pulling water from nearby sources, and breathing underwater.
- Breathing underwater: Percy can survive indefinitely underwater and communicate with sea creatures.
- Healing in water: Percy heals rapidly when in contact with water. Immersing himself in water restores his strength and closes wounds.
- Talking to horses: Poseidon created horses, so Percy can communicate with horses and pegasi.
- Controlling ships: Percy has an instinctive ability to navigate and control sea vessels.
- ADHD battle reflexes: Percy's hyperawareness makes him an exceptional fighter, reacting to threats faster than most demigods.
- The Curse of Achilles: In The Last Olympian, Percy bathes in the River Styx, making his body nearly invulnerable except for one small spot on his body (his Achilles heel, located on the small of his back).

== Riptide (Anaklusmos) ==
Percy's primary weapon is a sword called Riptide, known in Ancient Greek as Anaklusmos. It is a Celestial Bronze sword, meaning it can harm monsters and gods but passes harmlessly through mortals. In its dormant form, Riptide is disguised as an ordinary ballpoint pen that Percy keeps in his pocket. When uncapped, it transforms instantly into a full-length Greek sword. If lost or destroyed, Riptide always returns to Percy's pocket. The pen-to-sword transformation is one of the most iconic elements of the series.

== Camp Half-Blood ==
Camp Half-Blood is a training facility for demigods located on Long Island, New York, hidden from the mortal world by magical borders. It is protected by the magical boundaries of Thalia's pine tree on Half-Blood Hill. Percy first arrives at camp at age 12 and it becomes his second home.

The camp is divided into cabins, one for each Olympian god. Percy is claimed by Poseidon and lives in Cabin Three, which was empty for years because Poseidon had sworn an oath with Zeus and Hades not to have any more demigod children after World War II (their children were too powerful and caused global conflicts). Percy's existence is technically a violation of this oath.

Camp activities include sword fighting, archery, climbing the climbing wall (which periodically erupts in lava), chariot racing, and capture the flag. Capture the flag is taken very seriously and doubles as combat practice.

== Key Relationships ==
Annabeth Chase is Percy's best friend and eventual girlfriend. She is the daughter of Athena, goddess of wisdom, and is one of the most intelligent demigods at camp. Annabeth has been at Camp Half-Blood since age 7 and dreams of becoming an architect. She and Percy start as competitive allies, develop a deep friendship, and eventually fall in love. Their relationship is central to both the Percy Jackson and Heroes of Olympus series.

Grover Underwood is Percy's best friend and a satyr — half human, half goat. Grover was assigned to watch over Percy at Yancy Academy and guide him to Camp Half-Blood. He is gentle, anxious, and deeply passionate about nature and protecting endangered species. Grover has a deep empathic connection with Pan, the god of the wild.

Luke Castellan is the son of Hermes and initially a friend and mentor at camp. He becomes the primary antagonist of the first series after turning against the Olympians, resentful of how the gods treat their children. His arc ends in redemption in The Last Olympian.

Thalia Grace is the daughter of Zeus and one of Percy's closest allies. She was turned into a pine tree by her father to save her life, and was later restored to human form. She becomes the lieutenant of the Hunters of Artemis.

Nico di Angelo is the son of Hades, making him one of the most powerful demigods alive. He can summon the dead, shadow-travel, and communicate with ghosts. Initially introduced as a young boy obsessed with Mythomagic (a card game), he becomes a brooding, complex ally.

Tyson is Percy's half-brother — a young Cyclops, also a son of Poseidon. Despite being a monster by nature, Tyson is gentle, loyal, and deeply devoted to Percy. He works in the forges of Hephaestus and builds weapons for Percy.

== The Great Prophecy ==
A prophecy from the Oracle of Delphi states: "A half-blood of the eldest gods shall reach sixteen against all odds, and see the world in endless sleep, the hero's soul, cursed blade shall reap, a single choice shall end his days, Olympus to preserve or raze." This prophecy hangs over Percy throughout the first series, as everyone believes it refers to him. Its resolution in The Last Olympian forms the climax of the original five-book series.

== Books – Percy Jackson and the Olympians ==
The Lightning Thief (2005): Percy discovers he is a demigod, is accused of stealing Zeus's master lightning bolt, and must travel across America to recover it and prevent a war between the gods.
The Sea of Monsters (2006): Percy and his friends venture into the Sea of Monsters (the Bermuda Triangle) to retrieve the Golden Fleece and save Camp Half-Blood.
The Titan's Curse (2007): Percy joins a quest to rescue Annabeth and the goddess Artemis, who have been captured by the Titans.
The Battle of the Labyrinth (2008): Percy navigates Daedalus's Labyrinth beneath the United States to prevent Kronos's army from invading Camp Half-Blood.
The Last Olympian (2009): The final battle against Kronos's forces. Percy bathes in the River Styx and leads the defense of Olympus in Manhattan.

== Kronos and the Titans ==
The main antagonist of the first series is Kronos, the Titan lord of time and Percy's greatest enemy. Kronos was the father of Zeus, Poseidon, and Hades, and was defeated and cut into pieces by his sons after the Titanomachy. His remains were scattered in Tartarus, but he reassembles himself by possessing the body of Luke Castellan. Kronos represents the ultimate threat to Olympus and the modern world order.

== The Heroes of Olympus ==
After the events of the first series, Percy appears in the follow-up series The Heroes of Olympus, where he discovers a parallel camp — Camp Jupiter — for Roman demigods in California. Percy has lost his memory and must navigate an unfamiliar world while a new prophecy, the Prophecy of Seven, unfolds. He works alongside new heroes including Jason Grace (son of Jupiter/Zeus), Hazel Levesque (daughter of Pluto/Hades), Frank Zhang (son of Mars/Ares), Leo Valdez (son of Hephaestus), and Piper McLean (daughter of Aphrodite).

== The Underworld and Tartarus ==
Percy has visited the Underworld multiple times. He first descends to the Underworld in The Lightning Thief to confront Hades. In The Mark of Athena (Heroes of Olympus), Percy and Annabeth fall into Tartarus — the deepest, most dangerous pit of the Underworld — and must find their way back. Their survival in Tartarus is considered one of the greatest feats any demigod has ever accomplished.

== Percy's Legacy and Cultural Impact ==
The Percy Jackson series has sold over 180 million copies worldwide. It has been credited with getting an entire generation of children interested in Greek mythology, reading, and the idea that learning differences like dyslexia and ADHD are not limitations but different kinds of strength. A Disney+ live-action television series premiered in December 2023, with Walker Scobell playing Percy Jackson. Rick Riordan has praised the show as a more faithful adaptation of the books compared to the earlier films.

== Famous Quotes and Catchphrases ==
Percy's narrative voice is casual and self-aware. He frequently uses phrases like "Seriously?" when confronted with absurd situations. He refers to monsters, gods, and magical creatures with a deadpan matter-of-factness that treats the extraordinary as routine. Percy often jokes about being terrible at school, about the gods being dysfunctional, and about the unfairness of being a demigod. His humor is a defense mechanism and a coping tool, but it never undermines his genuine courage.
